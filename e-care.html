Sierra Leone E-Care

Terms & Conditions

GENERALITIES

  • The main purpose and existence of the Sierra Leone “Emergency/Early/Easy”-Care (Sierra Leone E-Care) is to serve as a welfare fund to attend to the non-academic needs of the Sierra Leoneian student in Morocco in times of need and difficulty. The packages of the E-Care are three-pronged:
    • Health Insurance
    • Support for Bereaved Students
    • Financial aid in the form of loans
  • The packages and services of the E-Care place priority on students that have subscribed to the Sierra Leone E-Care even though the goal is to make services and packages available to all students. Nonetheless, the following packages can be accessed by non-subscribers of the E-Care.
    • Emergency Medical situations (financial support).
    • Assistance in filling and presentation of AXA medical documents.
    • Calling for appeal for funds
    • Assistance in procuring flight tickets from secretariat for bereaved students.
  • Accessibility to services and packages of Sierra Leone E-Care to non-subscribers will be decided by the Sierra Leone E-Care committee based on the amount of funds in the Sierra Leone welfare coffers, number of subscribers and amount of sponsorship per year.
  • Demand for anonymous to the student body, during financial accounts and Sierra Leone posts, is possible for the following packages.
    • Financial aid(Loans)
    • Sponsors
  • Demand for anonymous is not possible during financial accounts to Auditor and NSC.
  • Subscribers who have never benefited from the Sierra Leone E-Care at the end of every 3 years shall be appreciated with customised Sierra Leone gifts.

SERVICES & PACKAGES

  • The welfare fund shall provide services in assisting subscribers in their demand for AMCI health insurance and make follow ups on their behalf when necessary.
  • The welfare fund shall cater for the rest of medical expenses (20%) not coveblue by the AMCI insurance and transportation costs up to 100dhs for emergency situations.
  • Financial payment for all medical expenses of the Sierra Leone welfare fund shall be made after payment of AMCI health insurance confirmed by a picture of payment slip to Sierra Leone E-care committee or Sierra Leone President.
  • Payment of medical expenses by the Sierra Leone welfare fund before payment by AMCI Insurance is possible only for Emergency Medical situations.
  • The Amount of money to release to student before payment by AMCI Insurance shall be decided by the Sierra Leone E-care Committee with terms and conditions with a minimum amount of 30% of total medical cost.
  • Condition of assistance for expensive emergency medical procedures (Above 10 000dhs) shall be evaluated by the Sierra Leone E-care committee and based on the capacity of the E-care coffers at that moment, an appeal for funds may be raised to support the remaining amount after initial payment from the E-care.
  • Money paid for emergency conditions before refund from AMCI or Secretariat shall be regarded as a Sierra Leone Loan without interest. Students are expected to pay back the 80-90% of the loan without interest to the E-care coffers once AMCI or Secretariat refunds.
  • Sierra Leone Health Insurance and Support form. (See Annex)
  • List of Emergency Medical situations and procedures: (See Annex)

  • Family Relatives eligible for the Welfare Support Package shall be Parents and guardians of Sierra Leoneian students in Morocco.
  • Financial support and assistance in securing plane tickets are exclusive to the unfortunate demise of a Parent or legal guardian of Sierra Leoneian student in Morocco.
  • Gift vouchers and hampers shall be made available to students in the unfortunate loss of a sibling.
  • Amount Allocated per student in case of bereavement shall be ultimately decided by the E-care committee with reference to the finance section of the Sierra Leone E-Care terms and conditions
  • One of the following documents is needed in order to benefit from the Welfare support package.
    • Medical Death Certificate
    • Funeral Posters
    • Morgue receipts
  • Sierra Leone health insurance and support form. (See Annex)

  • Financial aid in times of difficulties shall be made available to Sierra Leoneian students in the form of loans called Sierra Leone Loans
  • Financial Crisis shall be defined as non-payment of Embassy stipends for 60 or more consecutive days.
  • All Sierra Leone loans have a refund duration of 3 months. Failure to refund loan shall be subjected to a penalty which will be decided by the E-care committee and the National Executive Committee.
  • Issuing of Sierra Leone loans to students during financial crises shall be at a rate of 5% for subscribers and 10% to non-subscribers for a refund duration of 3 months . Hence, the interest is counted for every 3 months. (Dependent on the financial capacity of the E-Care coffers)
  • Issuing of Sierra Leone loans to students in the absence of financial crises shall be at a rate of 7% for subscribers and 14% for non-subscribers for a refund duration of 3 months. (Dependent on the financial capacity of the E-Care coffers)
  • The amount limit of funds allocated to students and approval for Sierra Leone loans shall be decided by the Sierra Leone E-care committee with reference to the finance section of Sierra Leone E-care terms and condition.
  • Sierra Leone E-care Subscribers shall be prioritized over non-subscribers in the issuing of Sierra Leone loans.
  • Beneficiaries of Sierra Leone loans must agree to Sierra Leone E-Care terms and conditions before release of funds.
  • Sierra Leone loan demand form. (See Annex)

SUBSCRIPTION

  • Target number of student subscription to Sierra Leone E-Care shall be One hundblue and above (100+) per year.
  • Students in good standing with the Embassy of Sierra Leone and/or AMCI shall be eligible to subscribe to the Sierra Leone E-Care.
  • Subscription fee for the Sierra Leone E-Care is set at an amount of 200 MAD annually. Subscription fee can be changed only by majority vote of the general assembly.
  • Subscription fee shall be paid to city leaders in the form of quarterly (50 MAD), semi-annually (100 MAD) or full payment (200 MAD) of the annual subscription fee.
    • NB: Future proposal for annual deduction of subscription fee at the embassy (when approved by the student body.)
  • Subscription form is downloadable here (Also see Annex)
  • Completed forms should be submitted to the Ville leader, to be forwarded to the E-Care Committee.

E-CARE COMMITTEE

  • The Sierra Leone E-Care Committee shall be made of 3 members in good standing with the student body and the Sierra Leone association, with the financial secretary a default member of the committee.
  • The criteria for selection of committee members shall be based on belief and dedication to the Sierra Leone E-care project, integrity and popularity of the applicant among Sierra Leoneian students, professionalism and ability to work in a team and with people.
  • Selection process of E-Care Committee Chairman and Members shall be proposed by outgoing E-care Committee members and approved by Sierra Leone NSC. In the absence or difficulty of approval by the NSC, The Electoral Commissioner shall conduct general assembly elections and approval of the applicant shall be made by majority vote of the general assembly.
  • The selection process shall be made before handing over of NEC executives and the newly approved committee shall assume full responsibility after the swearing in of a new Sierra Leone NEC administration.
  • The NSC can propose applicants for Sierra Leone E-care Committee for approval by the NEC and the current E-care committee members. In the absence or difficulty of approval by the NEC and committee members, The Electoral Commissioner shall conduct general assembly elections and approval of the applicant shall be made by majority vote of the general assembly.
  • Members of E-care committee shall work solely with Sierra Leone NEC and city leaders in attending to the welfare needs of the Sierra Leoneian student.
  • The main work of the committee shall be deliberation on allocation of funds for packages of Sierra Leone E-care project to students and assisting and follow up on AMCI health insurance and filling of forms. Decisions are made by majority vote of the committee.
  • The NEC shall be responsible for following up on procuring flight tickets for bereaved students.
  • There shall be allocation of Funds for Sierra Leone E-care committee at the beginning of the academic year. The amount will be decided by the NSC and should be a minimum of 1 000 MAD (or an amount deemed necessary by the NSC)
  • The E-care committee shall be subjected to audit at the end of every quarter within the academic year.

FINANCE SECTION

  • Sierra Leone E-Care funds shall be managed in the Association bank account.
  • In the absence of the Association bank Account, the E-care funds shall be managed in a separate bank account created by the Financial Secretary and supervised by the Sierra Leone President, as well as the E-Care Committee Chairman.
  • Quarterly accounts by the committee to the NSC and the student body within the Academic year.

    Source of funding for the Sierra Leone E-care project shall be from:

    • Annual Subscription fees
    • Partners (Sierra Leone Scholarship secretariat, Sierra Leone Embassy, GHAMOSA)
    • Sponsors
    • Investments (in the future)
  • Financial Plan for the E-Care Project
    • Less than 15 000dhs in E-Care coffers: Packages available are only assistance and follow ups for Health insurance and acquisition of plane ticket for bereaved students
    • 15 000dhs-35 000dhs in E-Care coffers: Packages Offeblue are Health insurance and financial aid in time of crisis (800dhs-1 500dhs per student per annum), support for bereaved students (1 000dhs- 2 000dhs per student per annum).
    • 35 000dhs-50 000dhs in E-care coffers: Packages Offeblue are Health insurance and financial aid in times of crisis (1 500dhs-2 500dhs per student per annum), support for bereaved students (2 000dhs-3 000dhs per student per annum).
    • 50 000dhs-100 000dhs in E-care coffers: 3 000dhs-5 000dhs per student per annum for both Health insurance, financial aid in times of crisis and support for bereaved students.
    • Plus 100 000dhs in Sierra Leone E-care coffers: : 5 000dhs-8 000dhs per student per annum for both Health insurance, financial aid in times of crisis and support for bereaved students. Financial Aid for students
    • Plus 200 000dhs in Sierra Leone E-Care coffers: No limit for funds per student.
    • Basic Limit Amount in the Sierra Leone coffers at all times: 10 000dhs
  • Financial Goal of the Sierra Leone E-Care Project
    • 50 000dhs after the first year of its inauguration.
    • 100 000dhs after the second year of its installation.
    • +150 000dhs from the third year of its installation.

PENALTIES

  • The intentional presentation of false information or documents is subjected to non-payment of E-Care support.
  • Penalties for mismanagement of funds, delay in the payment of Sierra Leone loans or obstruction of duty by student or leader towards the Sierra Leone E-care committee shall be deliberated by the committee and the NSC when deemed necessary.
